René Warren

genome scientist

 

Membrane protein crystallization

under microgravity, aboard

space shuttle Endeavour

R. Warren FL 1995

In his career René has been at the forefront of innovation, pioneering a number of bioinformatic “firsts”. Among those, SSAKE, first algorithm for genome assembly with short DNA sequences. Its algorithms are core to many genomics applications & inspiring new technologies still.


It found applications in T-cell profiling, HLA typing, structural variant detection in cancer, genome scaffolding, proteome assembly and was key to the discovery of Fusobacterium in colon cancer, one of the top 10 medical breakthroughs of 2011, as designated by Time magazine.
