René Warren

genome scientist

 

In his career, he has been at the forefront of innovation, pioneering a number of bioinformatics “firsts”. Among those the first DNA ‘short read’ de novo genome assembler, with algorithms core to many genomics applications & inspiring new technologies still.













These technologies find utility in T-cell profiling, HLA typing, structural variant detection in cancer, genome scaffolding, proteome assembly and were key to the colon cancer-fusobacterium discovery; Time magazine’s top 10 medical breakthroughs.
