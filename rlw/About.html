René Warren

genome scientist

 

In his career, he has been at the forefront of innovation, pioneering a number of bioinformatics “firsts”. Among those, SSAKE, the first DNA sequence ‘short read’ de novo genome assembler. Its algorithms are core to many genomics applications & inspiring new technologies still.














These technologies find applications in T-cell profiling, HLA typing, structural variant detection in cancer, genome scaffolding, proteome assembly and were key to the Fusobacteria in colon cancer discovery; Time magazine’s top 10 medical breakthroughs.
