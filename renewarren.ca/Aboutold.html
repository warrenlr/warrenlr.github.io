RenéWarren.ca

 

René is a senior bioinformatician with extensive experience in computational biology and a regular reviewer for scientific journals Bioinformatics & BMC bioinformatics.






He developed SSAKE, the first algorithm for genome assembly with short DNA sequences. Algorithms of SSAKE are the core of many genomics applications1 and their design continues to inspire new-generation assemblers2. Applications of SSAKE extend beyond genome assembly and the technology was applied to profiling T-cell metagenomes, targeted de novo assembly, HLA typing and was key to the discovery of Fusobacterium in colon cancer.
